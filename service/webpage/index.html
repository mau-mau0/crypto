Arbitrage

Arbitrage is a term that is thrown around a lot in the financial world. Many people think it's this magical thing that only Wall Street bankers can do. While that may be partially true, arbitrage opportunities are all around us and they're easy to take advantage of if you have the right tools. Especially in the inefficient crypto markets.

I built this application to make it easier for me to spot these opportunities. Instead of analyzing charts, and prices, and parsing through information myself, I built this to do it for me. My goal is to spot these opportunities and execute them using my developed strategies.

Strategies

Future-Spot Arbitrage

Future-spot arbitrage is a strategy that takes advantage of the discrepancy between an underlying asset's spot price and futures price. A trader can profit by simultaneously acquiring a long position in the secondary market and a short position in the futures market. By doing this, they can capture the difference in prices between the two markets and earn a risk-free profit.

I developed a service that tracks the spot and future price of a specific cryptocurrency and sends an alert when the spread reaches a predetermined level. Theoretically, this would execute a trade that longs the undervalued asset and shorts the overvalued asset.

Triangle-Trade Arbitrage

Triangle trade arbitrage occurs when there are discrepancies in currency exchange rates. It can happen when a particular currency is overvalued compared to one currency but undervalued compared to the other. Or, to put it in other words, the exchange between currencies in the market is inefficient. A trader can take advantage of this by placing three simultaneous trades, buying one currency and selling another, using a third currency as the base currency.

Visual Representation of Bellman-Ford

I have developed a service that implements the bellman-ford algorithm, which is generally used to find the shortest path between nodes. The algorithm loops through a given graph until it finds the shortest path unless a negative weight cycle is detected. A negative weight cycle would result in an infinite loop equaling an arbitrage opportunity when you involve currencies. This allows me to find opportunities in the market by identifying potential paths between currencies and detecting any cycles that could create an arbitrage situation.

Taking a look at figure 3, I drew a visual that outlines an example of an opportunity between three cryptocurrencies. Although there are five currencies in the visual where are going to use 3 for simplicity's sake. I built a script that feeds websocket data through the bellman-ford algorithm that spits out currency paths that create an arbitrage opportunity. Theoretically, it would take those trades and execute them making a small profit.

Explanation

In figure 3 the exchange rates of
Monero to Bitcoin is 0.741,
Bitcoin to Ethereum is 1.366,
Ethereum to Bitcoin is 0.995
Multiplication of these exchange rates = 0.741 * 1.366 * 0.995 = 1.007
Which greater than 1 making a 0.71% return.
